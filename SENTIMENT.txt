I conducted a research study on multimodal sentiment intelligence that compared traditional NLP methods, LSTM-based models, and transformer-based models — with a focus on evaluating how well each approach captures sentiment from text (and complementary signals). I trained and evaluated BERT on the Amazon reviews dataset and found that transformer-based contextual embeddings substantially outperformed LSTM and classical NLP baselines in classification metrics. Based on the experiments and ablation studies, BERT consistently gave the best representation for sentiment tasks, while GPT-style models were used only for generative tasks — e.g., producing natural-language explanations or example feedback based on the detected sentiment. The work culminated in a paper published in IJIRT where we report the experimental setup, evaluation metrics (accuracy, precision/recall/F1), and an analysis showing why contextual transformers edge out sequential models on this problem.
